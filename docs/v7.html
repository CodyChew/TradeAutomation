V7 Research Snapshot

V7 Entry Wait: Cancel At Would-Be 1R

Rejected

Decision Brief

V7 result: reject the cancel-first 1R entry-wait rule.

  • Fixed 6-bar baseline: 13,012 trades, 58.0% win rate, 0.116R avg, PF 1.265, 1,512.3R.
  • V7 cancel-first: 4,885 trades, 35.7% win rate, -0.321R avg, PF 0.519.
  • The cancel-first same-bar rule removes too many valid pullback entries.
  • This is not a drawdown improvement tradeoff; it is a materially worse model.

Next: Do not use V7; keep the fixed 6-bar pullback wait.

What This Tests

Should a pullback order stay open until entry or be cancelled once the would-be 1R target is reached first?

Setup Tested

Same 0.5 pullback, structure stop, 1R target model, but pending entry waits until either the pullback fills or the would-be 1R target is reached. If both happen in one candle, V7 cancels first.

How To Read This Page

This is not a new baseline. It is a rule challenge against the fixed 6-bar pullback wait.

Use / Do Not Use

Do not use V7 for strategy selection. Keep the fixed 6-bar entry wait.

Conclusion: Do not replace the fixed 6-bar pullback wait. V7 is unusable because the cancel-first same-bar rule removes too many valid trades.

Metric Guide

Start with Avg R and PF, then confirm the sample size with Trades. Bars is duration, not profit.
MetricHow To Read It
Avg RAverage net result per trade in risk units. This is the first metric to check.
Total RSum of net R across all trades in the row. Useful, but high-trade-count groups dominate it.
PFProfit factor: gross wins divided by gross losses. Above 1.0 means profitable before deeper checks.
Win RateShare of positive trades. It must be read together with target size and Avg R.
BarsAverage candles held per trade. On H4, 5 bars is about 20 hours; on D1, 5 bars is about 5 days.
TradesNumber of simulated trades. More trades give more confidence; tiny samples can mislead.
SkippedSignal/candidate attempts that did not become trades, usually because midpoint entry was not reached or risk was too wide.
