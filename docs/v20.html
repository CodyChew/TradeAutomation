V20 Protection Realism

M30 replay study

Decision Brief

V20 result: TP-near protection needs finer timing evidence before live design.

  • M30 replay control: 12,022 trades, 336.9R, PF about 1.058.
  • Same-M30 assumed upper bound: 512.9R, +176.0R versus control, but this relies on unknown intra-M30 ordering.
  • Conservative later-M30 activation did not beat control: m30_next was -53.0R and m30_delay1 was -5.5R.
  • Missed stop modifications are baseline-equivalent; successful modifications can still sacrifice later full TPs. Real 30-second live behavior needs M1/tick or forward attribution.

Next: Keep live TP/SL unchanged. If this remains a priority, collect M1/tick replay data or forward live attribution for 0.9R touches, stop-modify success, too-fast misses, and later outcomes.

What This Tests

Does the V19 locked-profit idea still work once live stop-modification timing is bracketed with M30 replay?

Setup Tested

Research-only lower-timeframe replay using the V15 LPFS signal baseline. H4/H8/H12/D1/W1 setups are replayed on M30 bid/ask candles to test lock-stop behavior at 0.9R with a 0.5R protected stop. Conservative variants only activate on a later M30 candle; the same-M30 assumed variant is an optimistic upper bound for the live 30-second loop.

How To Read This Page

Start with the Decision Card and Protection Funnel. The funnel separates 0.9R trigger touches, successful stop activations, too-fast misses, broker min-stop-distance blocks, and the optimistic same-M30 upper bound.

Use / Do Not Use

Use this page to decide whether a separate live stop-modification plan is justified. Do not change VPS, MT5 orders, live state, Telegram lifecycle, or production stop handling from V20 alone.

Conclusion: V20 says the idea is timing-sensitive. Same-M30 assumed activation improves the M30 replay control, but conservative later-M30 activation does not. This is not enough to change live behavior without M1/tick or forward evidence.

Rules Tested

  • Signal baseline stays V15/V13: LP3, H4/H8/H12/D1/W1, 0.5 signal-candle pullback, FS structure stop, 1R target, 6-bar entry window.
  • M30 replay finds the first bid/ask entry touch inside the same 6-bar higher-timeframe entry window.
  • The default stress variants never move the stop inside the same M30 candle; activation begins on a later M30 candle.
  • The same-M30 assumed variant is an optimistic upper bound for the live 30-second loop. It can show whether the idea deserves M1/tick validation, but it is not direct live evidence.
  • If price retreats too far before activation, the stop update is rejected as too late and the original bracket remains.
  • Optional min-stop-distance variants require current price to be at least a spread multiple away from the proposed 0.5R stop.
  • Same-bar stop/target conflict remains stop-first.

Metric Glossary

Realized DD
Closed-trade equity drawdown only. It ignores open trade risk until a position exits.
Risk-Reserved DD
Live-account stress drawdown that subtracts full open risk while positions are active.
Max Reserved Risk
Largest total open risk reserved at one time across accepted portfolio trades.
Worst Month
Largest calendar-month loss in account-percent terms for the tested schedule.
Return/DD
Total return divided by risk-reserved drawdown where available; higher is more efficient.
Avg R / PF
Average net R per trade and profit factor. Use both with trade count before trusting a row.
