V10 Portfolio Baseline: Take-All vs Exposure Caps

Portfolio study

Decision Brief

V10 result: LP3 cap 4R is the practical portfolio baseline.

  • LP3 take-all produced 1,512.3R, but breached the 30R drawdown guardrail with 33.4R max DD.
  • LP3 cap 4R produced 1,100.9R, 26.7R max DD, and 162D underwater, passing both guardrails.
  • LP4 and LP5 kept attractive PF, but their underwater periods were too long under the 180D rule.
  • The 4R cap sacrifices some total R, but it makes the model portfolio-feasible under the current guardrails.

Next: Use LP3 cap 4R for timeframe-mix testing.

What This Tests

Does a practical portfolio exposure cap improve drawdown and underwater behavior enough to replace taking every LP + Force Strike trade?

Setup Tested

Existing V9 trades only. LP3, LP4, and LP5 are tested across all H4/H8/H12/D1/W1 trades. V10 compares take-all against 4R, 6R, 8R, and 10R max-open-risk caps with one open trade per symbol.

How To Read This Page

Start with Best Practical Mechanics. A row must pass 30R max drawdown and 180D longest underwater before total R is allowed to choose the winner.

Use / Do Not Use

Use LP3 cap 4R as the current practical portfolio baseline. Move next to V11 timeframe mix testing using this exposure rule.

Conclusion: Only LP3 with the 4R exposure cap passed both V10 guardrails. Take-all had higher Total R, but breached the 30R max drawdown guardrail. LP4 and LP5 kept attractive quality metrics, but their underwater periods were too long under the 180D rule.

Metric Glossary

Realized DD
Closed-trade equity drawdown only. It ignores open trade risk until a position exits.
Risk-Reserved DD
Live-account stress drawdown that subtracts full open risk while positions are active.
Max Reserved Risk
Largest total open risk reserved at one time across accepted portfolio trades.
Worst Month
Largest calendar-month loss in account-percent terms for the tested schedule.
Return/DD
Total return divided by risk-reserved drawdown where available; higher is more efficient.
Avg R / PF
Average net R per trade and profit factor. Use both with trade count before trusting a row.
