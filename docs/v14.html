V14 Risk Sizing And Drawdown

Risk sizing guide

What This Tests

What drawdowns should be expected when the V13 take-all baseline is converted from R results into fixed account-risk and timeframe-ladder risk schedules?

Setup Tested

Existing V9 trade rows only. The strategy model stays fixed: LP3, all H4/H8/H12/D1/W1, take-all handling, 0.5 signal-candle pullback, full Force Strike structure stop, single 1R target, and fixed 6-bar pullback wait.

How To Read This Page

Start with the Decision Brief and Recommendation Card. Realized drawdown uses closed trade exits, while risk-reserved drawdown subtracts full open risk while trades are active and is the more conservative live-account stress view.

Use / Do Not Use

Use the balanced equal-LTF ladder as the first live-style sizing candidate. Fixed 0.25% is the closest simple alternative; fixed 0.50% is a high-return, high-stress diagnostic rather than the first practical default.

Conclusion: The balanced equal-LTF ladder is the practical starting risk schedule. It keeps H4 and H8 equal, gives higher risk to H12/D1/W1, and produces a moderate stress profile: about 332.6% total return, 6.2% realized max DD, 8.6% risk-reserved max DD, and 5.7% max reserved open risk.

Decision Brief

V14 result: use the balanced equal-LTF ladder as the practical starting risk schedule.

  • Balanced equal-LTF: total +332.6%, realized DD 6.2%, risk-reserved DD 8.6%, worst month -3.6%, max reserved open risk 5.7%.
  • Fixed 0.25%: total +378.1%, realized DD 8.3%, risk-reserved DD 9.8%, worst month -4.1%; simpler, but it does not upweight the cleaner higher timeframes.
  • Fixed 0.50%: total +756.1%, risk-reserved DD 19.5%, worst week -10.2%, worst month -8.1%; useful as a stress diagnostic, not the first practical default.
  • Conservative equal-LTF: total +240.3%, risk-reserved DD 6.7%, max open risk 4.2%; smoother if drawdown tolerance is lower.

Next: Use V14 to choose account risk, then test account-level constraints such as daily loss, max loss, and execution limits.
