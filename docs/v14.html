How To Read This Page

Start with the Decision Brief and Recommendation Card. Realized drawdown uses closed trade exits, while risk-reserved drawdown subtracts full open risk while trades are active and is the more conservative live-account stress view.

Use / Do Not Use

Use the tight H12-D1 basket as the first live-style sizing candidate. Balanced equal-LTF is the growth-tilted alternative; fixed 0.25% is the closest simple fixed-risk option; fixed 0.50% is a high-return, high-stress diagnostic.

Conclusion: The tight H12-D1 basket is the current practical risk schedule. It keeps H4 and H8 equal, groups H12 and D1 together, keeps W1 higher, and slightly improves drawdown/open-risk efficiency versus the balanced ladder while giving up only a small amount of total return.

Metric Glossary

Realized DD
Closed-trade equity drawdown only. It ignores open trade risk until a position exits.
Risk-Reserved DD
Live-account stress drawdown that subtracts full open risk while positions are active.
Max Reserved Risk
Largest total open risk reserved at one time across accepted portfolio trades.
Worst Month
Largest calendar-month loss in account-percent terms for the tested schedule.
Return/DD
Total return divided by risk-reserved drawdown where available; higher is more efficient.
Avg R / PF
Average net R per trade and profit factor. Use both with trade count before trusting a row.
