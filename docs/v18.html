V18 TP-Near Exit Research

TP-near study

Decision Brief

V18 result: TP-near exits have potential, but need review before any live change.

  • No-buffer bid/ask control: 12,917 trades, 1,535.2R, 0.119R avg, PF 1.270.
  • Best variant close_pct_90: 2,250.7R, 0.174R avg, PF 1.426, a +715.5R delta versus the control.
  • close_pct_90 saved 390 trades from later stops for about +741.0R, but sacrificed 658 full TPs for about -65.8R.
  • This is still research-only. It does not touch MT5 live orders, pending-order lifecycle, market recovery, or Telegram execution behavior.

Next: Inspect the changed-trade rows by symbol/timeframe and decide whether the next experiment should validate close_pct_90, a locked-profit variant, or a stricter volatility-aware TP-near rule.

What This Tests

Should LPFS close or protect trades when bid/ask price gets very close to the 1R target before the full target is filled?

Setup Tested

Research-only TP-near exit study using the V16 bid/ask simulator as the control environment. The trade model stays LP3, H4/H8/H12/D1/W1, 0.5 signal-candle pullback, FS structure stop, 1R target, fixed 6-bar signal window, and fixed 6-bar pullback wait. Variants test immediate close, breakeven protect, and locked-profit protect at percent-to-target and spread-multiple thresholds.

How To Read This Page

Start with Decision Read, then compare each TP-near variant against the no-buffer bid/ask control and canonical V15. Use the outcome breakdown to separate saved stops from sacrificed full TPs before considering any live change.

Use / Do Not Use

Keep live behavior unchanged. Review the changed-trade samples and robustness by symbol/timeframe before deciding whether TP-near close or protect logic deserves a separate live-change plan.

Conclusion: The TP-near idea has promise but is not a live-rule change yet. The 90% immediate-close variant materially improved the research control, mainly by saving trades from later stops, while still sacrificing some full TPs.

Rules Tested

  • Control uses V16 no-buffer bid/ask mechanics, not a live behavior change.
  • Long TP-near uses Bid; short TP-near uses Ask.
  • Immediate-close variants exit at the threshold price when reached.
  • Protect variants activate breakeven or locked-R stop after the near-TP bar; full TP can still hit later.
  • Same-bar stop/target conflict remains stop-first.

Metric Glossary

Realized DD
Closed-trade equity drawdown only. It ignores open trade risk until a position exits.
Risk-Reserved DD
Live-account stress drawdown that subtracts full open risk while positions are active.
Max Reserved Risk
Largest total open risk reserved at one time across accepted portfolio trades.
Worst Month
Largest calendar-month loss in account-percent terms for the tested schedule.
Return/DD
Total return divided by risk-reserved drawdown where available; higher is more efficient.
Avg R / PF
Average net R per trade and profit factor. Use both with trade count before trusting a row.
