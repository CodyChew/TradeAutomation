V8 Entry Wait: Entry-Priority 1R Cancel

Rejected

Decision Brief

V8 result: positive, but still weaker than the baseline.

  • Fixed 6-bar baseline: 13,012 trades, 58.0% win rate, 0.116R avg, PF 1.265, 1,512.3R.
  • V8 entry-first: 9,627 trades, 55.3% win rate, 0.062R avg, PF 1.133, 598.3R.
  • Entry-first handling fixes the worst V7 flaw, but the 1R-cancel wait still cuts too much profitable opportunity.
  • The fixed 6-bar wait remains better on trade count, win rate, average R, PF, and total R.

Next: Do not replace the fixed 6-bar pullback wait.

What This Tests

If a candle touches both pullback entry and would-be 1R, does entry-first handling make the 1R-cancel wait rule usable?

Setup Tested

Same test as V7, but if entry and would-be 1R occur in the same candle, the setup fills first instead of cancelling first.

How To Read This Page

Use / Do Not Use

Closed-trade equity drawdown only. It ignores open trade risk until a position exits.

Metric Guide

Start with Avg R and PF, then confirm the sample size with Trades. Bars is duration, not profit.
MetricHow To Read It
Avg RAverage net result per trade in risk units. This is the first metric to check.
Total RSum of net R across all trades in the row. Useful, but high-trade-count groups dominate it.
PFProfit factor: gross wins divided by gross losses. Above 1.0 means profitable before deeper checks.
Win RateShare of positive trades. It must be read together with target size and Avg R.
BarsAverage candles held per trade. On H4, 5 bars is about 20 hours; on D1, 5 bars is about 5 days.
TradesNumber of simulated trades. More trades give more confidence; tiny samples can mislead.
SkippedSignal/candidate attempts that did not become trades, usually because midpoint entry was not reached or risk was too wide.
